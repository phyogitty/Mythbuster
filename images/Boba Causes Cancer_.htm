Boba Causes Cancer?

What is Boba?


Boba is a popular topping in many tea and other beverages(although it is always seen sinking at the bottom of the cup)

It is widely found in milk tea, also called "bubble tea", "pearl tea" or simply "boba tea," the cold drink originated from Taiwan.

The most consumed boba has black color, and the texture is soft and chewy.

What is boba made of?

Boba is made of white tapioca starch which comes from cassava root.

The boba itself is tasteless.

Tapioca starch balls used in the drinks are usually dyed black or other colors for aesthetic appeal and coated with sugar for sweetness.

What is tapioca starch exactly?

Tapioca starch is ,again, extracted from cassava root; cassava plants are originated from Brazil

It is pure carbohydrates; fat and protein free

It is also gluten free; therefore, mainly used in gluten free food products

Tapioca starch is a powerful thickening agent; used in from soup and gravy to candies and desserts and our main discussion here, boba!

Boba causes cancer?

The boba drinks widely spreaded across Asia, the boba shops has multiplied over time in North America, as well as it got famous in the Europe, especially Germany.

The myth about boba started with the release of the research from University Hospital Aachen, Germany.

What was the finding? According to the report of the German researcher, Manfred Möller, of the Institute of Hygiene and Environmental Medicin at Aachen, the tapioca contain stryrene, acetophenone and other unspecified brominated chemicals.

These chemicals, they said, "shouldn't be in food at all" and they are considered by those German researchers and the media to relate to polychlorinated biphenyls(PCB), which reportedly can cause cancer and other harmful health effects in animals and humans.

Many secondary reports among the foreign mass media, and cancer-related concerns among the boba drink consumers arise from that finding from Germany. The news also concerned parents because their teenager kids are loving and drinking various kinds of boba beverages.

Myth Busted!
* Boba does not cause cancer *

(1) The study by German researchers is flawed.
  • UC Berkeley assessed that there was no official publication, and thus the finding was not peer-reviewed.
  • Online media, Daily News, reports that "Samples were taken from an unnamed chain in Mönchengladbach, in northwest Germany and the tapioca balls were made in Taiwan."
  • The quantity of boba was not also specified.
  • All of the above imply the lack of reliablity of German study on tapioca/boba.


(2) Under Taiwanese government's authority, the Cosumer Protection Comittee tested and confirmed that tapioca pearls are safe to consume.
  • They tested 22 samples from 7 manufacturers in Taiwan
  • They did not find styrene and acetophenone in all samples
  • However, unlike German researchers, they specifically found brominated biphenyls.
  • According to the statement on Polybrominated Biphenyls (PBBs) by Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry, "the amounts of PBBs that caused health effects in animals are much greater than levels of PBBs normally found in the environment." That means that those chemicals cannot be easily harmful to humans.


(3) US FDA (United States Food and Drug Administration) states that styrene and acetophenone are not PCB.
  • In fact, FDA approved styrene and acetophenone to be used in food production.
  • They are not chemically related to PCB as well.

Molecular Structures of Styrene, Polychlorinated Biphenyls, and Acetophenone
Let's learn more about the chemicals which are claimed to be in what boba is made out of in order to reduce our health concerns

Styrene

Styrene is a colorless liquid that evaporates easily, and it has a sweet smell. Styrene is naturally found in foods such as coffee beans, strawberries, cinnamon and peanuts. Polymerised styrene(in solid state) are used in the manufacture of plastic containers and packaging materials for vehicles.

>>> A small amount of styrene that we may be exposed through inhalation and consumption of food is not harmful to our bodies. Many studies and experiments have shown that styrene is not associated with severe health risk, including cancer.

Next time, if any faulty studies asserted that styrene was in the food you love like German research claimed about boba in popular milk tea, you ca be less panicked and less concerned by that chemical. Polychlorinated Biphenyls

PCBs are a group of synthetic chemicals. They were massively produced in US and Europe. However, the manufacture of PCBs is now highly banned in many countries due to their potential dangerous impact on humans' health and environment.

PCBs are hardly soluble in water, but easily in fat. PCBs are especially found in animal fats, which may be in our food chain. They can also build up by contamination in harvested fish and other source of food.

>>> Therefore, since boba is made of tapioca starch, which mostly lack fat as mentioned above, there is no or little chance that PCBs can build up in boba unless tapioca starch, the source of ingredient for boba, is somehow contaminated. In fact, we can be free from concerns as long as we eat good amount of non-contaminated food. Acetophenone

Acetophenone is a colorless, viscous liquid and it has sweet smell and taste. Like styrene, it also can be found naturally in fruits and vegetables, such as apple, banana and cauliflower; and also cheese and beef. The chemical is used in many industrial production, for example, resin, plastics, fragance like soap and perfumes, and some pharmaceuticals.

>>> U.S. Environmental protection agency labeled acetophenone as a Group D carcinogen; which means that it does not take part in any cancer development in human body.

If we are familiar with the chemical, we can be free from having second thoughs about enjoying our favorite food after hearing rumor-like scientific information.
